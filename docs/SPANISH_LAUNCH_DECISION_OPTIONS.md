# Spanish Launch Decision Options

## Purpose
This document frames the remaining strategic choice for Spanish after corpus remediation, audio refresh, runtime wiring, and launch-surface gating.

The question is not whether Spanish is real enough to matter. It is. The question is how broadly to position it at launch, and where the remaining ElevenLabs credit budget should go.

Current monthly remaining ElevenLabs budget:
- approximately `260,000` credits

## What Is Already True
Spanish now has launch-capable coverage in the product for:
- detection
- sentences
- conversations
- phoneme drills
- scenarios

Spanish does **not** yet have full parity for:
- placement assessment
- stories
- word-pair track
- gross discrimination track
- category practice
- fully localized app chrome

That means the strategic decision is a scope-positioning choice, not a raw content-existence choice.

## Option 1: Scoped Core Launch Track
### Definition
Launch Spanish publicly as a first-class but scoped track covering the clinically strongest activities already ready in the app:
- detection
- sentences
- conversations
- drills
- scenarios

Position it as a serious core listening pathway, not full English parity.

### Pros
- fastest path to launch with current assets
- clinically strongest path because it uses the content already remediated and quality-gated
- avoids wasting credits on lower-value filler just to claim parity
- cleaner product story: Spanish is available now for core rehabilitation tasks
- lets real users generate outcome data sooner

### Cons
- some Spanish users will notice missing English-side activities
- marketing and onboarding language has to be precise to avoid implying full parity
- no true Spanish placement assessment at launch
- some UI/chrome remains English

### Credit Use
Best use of the remaining `260k` credits under this option:
1. reserve a remediation buffer for any listening-QC failures
2. expand Spanish scenarios and high-transfer comprehension sets
3. create a small Spanish placement starter set only if time remains

Recommended spend pattern:
- `40k-60k` held as correction buffer
- `120k-160k` on additional Spanish scenario/comprehension content
- `40k-80k` on a lightweight Spanish placement starter or extra detection/noise variants

### Assessment
This is the best launch option if the goal is forward motion without diluting clinical rigor.

## Option 2: Spanish Public Beta / Soft Launch
### Definition
Expose Spanish to invited users, referrals, or a controlled segment first, while calling it beta and keeping marketing softer.

### Pros
- lowers reputational risk while human listening QC and UI polish continue
- gives time to observe Spanish retention, error patterns, and activity completion before broad promotion
- creates space to refine placement and localization based on actual user behavior
- allows careful use of remaining credits after observing real friction points

### Cons
- slower revenue and slower signal at the top of funnel
- weaker market statement than a true launch
- requires discipline to avoid the beta state lingering too long
- still needs product messaging clarity and operational monitoring

### Credit Use
Best use of the remaining `260k` credits under this option:
1. hold most credits until early beta data arrives
2. spend only on the highest-confidence missing pieces that beta confirms

Recommended spend pattern:
- `80k-120k` held back pending beta data
- `60k-100k` on Spanish placement or onboarding-support content
- `40k-80k` on scenario/comprehension expansion after beta feedback

### Assessment
This is the safest option if you want more launch confidence, but it costs momentum and delays clearer market learning.

## Option 3: Full Parity Push Before Public Launch
### Definition
Delay broader Spanish launch until the app has near-English breadth, including at least some combination of:
- Spanish placement
- Spanish stories
- Spanish word-pair track
- Spanish gross discrimination or category expansions
- more fully localized chrome and labels

### Pros
- strongest parity narrative in marketing
- fewer visible scope gaps for Spanish-first users
- more polished impression if Spanish is a central acquisition channel from day one

### Cons
- slowest path to launch
- highest credit burn risk
- easiest way to spend heavily on lower-yield content before real user evidence exists
- likely to expand engineering scope beyond content into broader UI/localization debt
- risks confusing “more features” with “better rehab outcomes”

### Credit Use
Best use of the remaining `260k` credits under this option:
1. Spanish placement content first
2. then any missing clinically important discrimination/identification tracks
3. only then stories or broader lifestyle content

Likely spend pattern:
- `70k-110k` on a dedicated Spanish placement flow and supporting audio
- `80k-120k` on additional track parity content
- `40k-80k` on polish or scenario breadth

### Assessment
This is the least efficient option unless Spanish parity is essential to the launch narrative. It is defensible only if Spanish is a primary acquisition wedge and you are willing to delay launch for breadth.

## Overall Recommendation
Choose **Option 1: Scoped Core Launch Track**.

Reasoning:
- it matches the current clinically strongest Spanish corpus
- it preserves momentum toward launch
- it uses credits on the highest-transfer content rather than parity theater
- it avoids turning launch into an open-ended localization program

Recommended launch framing:
- Spanish is available now for core listening rehabilitation
- launch scope includes detection, sentences, conversations, drills, and scenarios
- additional Spanish modules will expand based on clinical priority and user demand

## Credit Recommendation
Given `260k` remaining credits, do **not** spend them all before launch.

Recommended immediate plan:
1. reserve `50k` for post-QC corrections and unavoidable re-renders
2. spend `120k-150k` on additional Spanish scenario/comprehension content because that is the most transferable and retention-relevant launch expansion
3. reserve the remainder for the first Spanish placement iteration after early user data or for selective post-launch expansion

## Launch Standard
The launch bar should be:
1. human bilingual listening QC complete
2. production app serving the current Spanish-aware launch surface
3. Spanish marketed as a scoped core track, not full parity
4. remaining credits deployed only where they improve clinical value or retention, not where they merely increase menu count
